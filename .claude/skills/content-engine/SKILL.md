# Content Engine

Most owners do not have a content problem ... they have a "what do I even post?" problem. This skill solves it: it turns the owner's Company Brain into a real content calendar and a first batch of posts that sound like them, mapped to who they are trying to reach.

## Using this inside the AI First Hire Kit

This is a **Day 4** skill. It works best when the Company Brain is filled in (Day 1) and the **Dream Customer Avatar** is built (Day 2). The research tool (**ScrapeCreators**) and images (**GPT Image 2 via FAL**) connect on Day 4 ... see [`TOOLS-SETUP.md`](../../../TOOLS-SETUP.md). Posts queue in **Product Champ → Marketing → Social Planner** (connected on Day 2).

## The two jobs content does

- **Trust content** ... for people who discover you and are checking you out (cold / warm). Builds authority and likability so they choose you.
- **Conversion content** ... for people actively searching for the problem right now (hot / commercial intent). Answers the question and moves them to act.

A healthy calendar is mostly trust content with regular conversion content woven in.

## Audience temperature (map every post to one)

- **Cold** ... they do not know you. Hook + a little value, no ask.
- **Warm** ... following, not ready. Teaching, proof, story.
- **Hot** ... they know they have the problem, have not pulled the trigger. Offer, case study, clear CTA.
- **Actively searching** ... commercial intent right now. Conversion content that answers and invites the next step.

## The five content types

1. **Hook** ... a pattern interrupt / scroll-stopper built on one idea.
2. **Core teaching** ... teach one useful thing well.
3. **Over-the-shoulder** ... show how you do the work (the process behind the result).
4. **Day-in-the-life** ... humanize the business; build know/like/trust.
5. **Trending** ... ride a relevant trend or format, on-brand only.

## Workflow

1. **Read the brain:** `company/overview.md`, `ideal-customer.md` (+ any `audience/` avatar pages), `voice-and-tone.md`, `products-and-services.md`, `proof.md`. You are writing as the owner, to their buyer.
2. **Research what's working:** use **ScrapeCreators** (and the ad library) to pull the hooks, formats, and topics performing in their niche and for competitors. Capture real angles, not generic ones. If ScrapeCreators is not connected, use whatever web research you have, or work from the brain.
3. **Build the calendar:** a 2-week plan (offer a 4-week option). Each slot = date, platform, content type, temperature, trust-or-conversion, hook, the idea, and the CTA. Balance the types and temperatures ... do not post all one thing. Match the cadence to what the owner can actually sustain (3 great posts a week beats 14 mediocre ones).
4. **Write the first week's batch** in the owner's voice (from `voice-and-tone.md`). Each post: a real hook, a tight body, and a CTA matched to the temperature. Human, specific, never slop.
5. **Images:** for posts that need a visual, generate one with **GPT Image 2 (via FAL)** ... on-brand, legible text. If FAL is not connected, write a clear image brief instead.
6. **Queue:** load the batch into **Product Champ → Marketing → Social Planner**, scheduled to the calendar. Show the owner before anything publishes.

## Output

- A clean **calendar table** the owner can scan at a glance.
- The **first week of posts**, ready to queue, in their voice.
- An **image (or image brief)** for each post that needs one.

## Guardrails

- Voice comes from the brain, always. If a post could be about any business, rewrite it until it could only be about this one.
- **Real proof only.** Never invent results or testimonials.
- Say "AI employee," never "bot." Use ellipses, not em-dashes. 7th-grade clarity.
- Show the owner before anything publishes. Their yes, every time, until they tell you to loosen it.
